<span id="page-95-4"></span><span id="page-95-3"></span>Keyboard shortcuts are used throughout Mac OS X to provide quick ways for users to initiate certain actions. Many are provided by the operating system to meet both general usability needs and accessibility needs. The operating system therefore reserves certain keys and keyboard combinations for its use. These combinations, listed in Table 7-3 and Table 7-4, affect all applications and should not be used for anyotherfunction. Otherkeyboard shortcuts are used bythe Universal Access features in Mac OS X and should be avoided. A complete list of the keyboard shortcuts commonly used in Mac OS X is provided in ["Keyboard](#page-300-0) Shortcuts Quick Reference" (page 301).  
You may also define keyboard shortcuts in your application for frequently used commands. Some guidelines on how to decide which shortcuts are appropriate are in "Creating Your Own [Keyboard](#page-96-2) [Shortcuts"](#page-96-2) (page 97). Other sections of this document suggestkeyboard shortcuts,where appropriate, to help you maintain a consistent and familiar user experience across applications.
